The Foundations: What is Calculus?

  • A branch of mathematics dealing with rates of change and accumulation.
  • Developed independently by Isaac Newton and Gottfried Leibniz in the 17th century.
  • Comprises two main branches: Differential Calculus and Integral Calculus.
  • Provides tools to model and solve problems involving continuous change.

Calculus is essentially the mathematics of change. Differential calculus focuses on instantaneous rates of change and slopes of curves, while integral calculus deals with accumulation, areas, and volumes.
